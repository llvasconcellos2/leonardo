Install the Jelly Bean 4.1.2 Stock Retail ROM. You can also use this procedure to unbrick your Razr if you tried to downgrade it.

1.  Download the stock ROM according to your device and region. I found the downloads on the [XDA forum](http://forum.xda-developers.com/showthread.php?t=2231289 "RAZR XT910 Firmwares SuperThread (Jelly Bean)"), and even though my device was originally a VIVO one, I had already unlocked it and preferred to install the retail version to avoid VIVO bloatware.
    
    Here is the [LINK](http://fwteam.grupoandroid.com/RAZR%20XT910/9.8.2O-124_SPUL-17_Retail_BR_SIGNED_S3_USASPYDRETBRB1B2B5B8B8LA038.0R_p2b_S1FF_fb.xml.zip "9.8.2O-124_SPUL-17_Retail_BR_SIGNED_S3_USASPYDRETBRB1B2B5B8B8LA038 .0R_p2b_S1FF_fb.xml.zip") to the file I downloaded from the [POST](http://forum.xda-developers.com/showthread.php?t=2292919 "Firmware RAZR XT910 Retail Brazil Jelly Bean 4.1.2").
    
    I also took the opportunity to upload the ROM to [4shared](http://www.4shared.com/zip/O20qirisce/982O-124_SPUL-17_Retail_BR_SIG.html "ROM Razr XT910 4.1.2 Jelly Bean").
2.  Download and install the Motorola drivers ([Motorola Device Manager](http://www.4shared.com/rar/_8dFBW7gba/MotorolaDeviceManager_234.html "Motorola Device Manager")).
3.  Download and install the [RSD Lite](http://www.4shared.com/file/A6G6VSYlce/RSDLite615.html "RSD Lite 6.15") software. RSD Lite is Motorola's software used to flash the ROM onto the device.
4.  If possible, do a factory reset on your phone before starting (Settings – Privacy).
5.  Put the device in fastboot mode: with the battery charged, or at least at 70%, and the USB cable disconnected, turn the device off and, while holding the volume + and volume – buttons, press the power button until the boot menu, or **Boot Mode Selection Menu**, appears. Use the volume – button to change the option and volume + to select. Select the **AP Fastboot** option as shown in the image below:
    
    [![Boot Mode Selection Menu](/old-blog/uploads/2014/01/IMG_0121.jpg)](/old-blog/uploads/2014/01/IMG_0121.jpg)
    
    Motorola Razr XT910 boot menu
    
6.  Start RSD Lite and, in File Name, select your ROM file. Mine is 9.8.2O-124\_SPUL-17\_Retail\_BR\_SIGNED\_S3\_USASPYDRETBRB1B2B5B8B8LA038.0R\_p2b\_S1FF\_fb.xml.zip. Select the Decompress Only option.
    
    [![Motorola RSD Lite](/old-blog/uploads/2014/01/motorola_rsdlite.jpg)](/old-blog/uploads/2014/01/motorola_rsdlite.jpg)
    
    Decompressing the ROM in RSD Lite
    
7.  Connect the USB cable to the device. The device will change from "Connect USB Data Cabe" to "Transfer Mode: USB Connected". RSD Lite will recognize the device with the status "Connected" as shown in the image:
    
    [![Motorola RSD Lite 6.15](/old-blog/uploads/2014/01/motorola_rsdlite_pronto.jpg)](/old-blog/uploads/2014/01/motorola_rsdlite_pronto.jpg)
    
    ROM loaded in Motorola RSD Lite.
    
8.  Click Start. The device will restart at least once, and at the end of the process the Result column will show the word "PASS", indicating that everything went well. Wait until the Android Welcome screen appears before removing the cable.
9.  Set up your device and you're done!
